## Multi-skill DID strategy

When you publish multiple paid skills, you have two options for how to model your DIDs:

| Strategy | Setup | Revenue view | Best for |
|----------|-------|-------------|----------|
| **One DID, many skills** | Create one wallet, register one DID, use it as `skill_did` for all skills | All revenue pools into one wallet. Query `/api/v1/sales?skill_did=...` returns all purchases across all skills. | Most developers — simplest to manage |
| **One DID per skill** | Create one wallet and DID per skill | Revenue is isolated per skill. Each has its own wallet balance and sales history. | Skills with different pricing tiers, co-authors, or separate accounting needs |

### One DID, many skills (recommended)

```
did:solana:AbCdEf...   ← your single developer DID
  ├── skill: "AI Writing Assistant"   ($5)
  ├── skill: "Data Analysis Tool"     ($10)
  └── skill: "Logo Generator"         ($3)
         All payments → same wallet
```

Pros: One wallet to manage, one DID to remember, simpler tax reporting.  
Cons: Can't separate revenue by skill at the wallet level (use `/api/v1/sales` to break it down).

### One DID per skill

```
did:solana:AbCdEf...   ← wallet for "AI Writing Assistant"
did:solana:XyZ123...   ← wallet for "Data Analysis Tool"
did:solana:QwErTy...   ← wallet for "Logo Generator"
         Payments → separate wallets
```

Pros: Clean revenue separation, per-skill wallets, easier to transfer/sell a skill.  
Cons: More wallets to manage, more DIDs to register, more env vars if signing for each.

<Info>
  You can change strategy later. Your existing DID keeps working — add new DIDs for new skills or consolidate. There's no migration — just start using the new DID for new skills.
</Info>

## Reconciliation

When a buyer pays for your skill, three identifiers are generated at different stages. Understanding what each one is and when it appears lets you match records across systems.

### The three identifiers

| Identifier | Generated by | When | Format | Where visible |
|-----------|-------------|------|--------|---------------|
| `tx_id` | Payment Service | `POST /api/v1/pay` response | UUID | `/api/v1/pay/{tx_id}`, `/api/v1/sales`, `/api/v1/verify` |
| `tx_hash` | Solana network | Chain confirmation | Base58 string (88 chars) | `/api/v1/pay/{tx_id}` (in `tx_hash` field), Solana Explorer |
| Purchase proof | Verification Service | RocketMQ event consumed | `proof_hash` (SHA256) | `/api/v1/verify/proof` (planned) |

### Reconciliation flow

```
1. POST /api/v1/pay returns tx_id ──────────────┐
2. Poll GET /api/v1/pay/{tx_id} until confirmed  │  Your system
3. tx_hash is now available ─────────────────────┘
4. Cross-check tx_hash on Solana Explorer ─────── External verification
5. GET /api/v1/sales shows the purchase ───────── Dashboard
6. GET /api/v1/verify confirms the record ─────── Backend enforcement
```

All three identifiers refer to the same payment. `tx_id` is your system-of-record key. `tx_hash` is the ground truth on Solana. Purchase proof is the cryptographic record.

### Matching records across systems

If numbers don't add up between your dashboard and your wallet:

```bash
# Step 1: Get a sales record
curl -H "X-API-Key: $STABLEPAY_API_KEY" \
  "https://ai.wenfu.cn/api/v1/sales?skill_did=did:solana:<yours>"

# Response includes tx_id for each sale

# Step 2: Get the tx_hash from the payment status
curl -H "X-StablePay-DID: ..." \
  "https://ai.wenfu.cn/api/v1/pay/<tx_id>"

# Step 3: Look up tx_hash on Solana Explorer (see below)
```

## Verify on Solana Explorer

Every StablePay payment is a real SPL Token transfer on Solana. You can verify any transaction independently — no trust in StablePay required.

### Using Solana Explorer

Take the `tx_hash` from `GET /api/v1/pay/{tx_id}` and open:

```
https://explorer.solana.com/tx/<tx_hash>?cluster=devnet
```

<Note>
  **Sandbox → DevNet.** Change `?cluster=devnet` to `?cluster=mainnet` for Production when Mainnet is live. Or just use `explorer.solana.com/tx/<tx_hash>` — the explorer auto-detects.
</Note>

### What to look for

The explorer shows:

- **Token transfers**: You'll see the SPL Token transfer from the buyer's wallet to yours. The amount should match the purchase price.
- **Fee payer**: The hotwallet address (not the buyer) paid the SOL gas fee — confirming the gas subsidy.
- **Timestamp**: Matches when the payment was confirmed on-chain, which should be within 30 seconds of when `POST /api/v1/pay` was called.
- **Status**: `Finalized` means the transaction is irreversible.

If the explorer shows a transfer to your wallet address matching the price and currency, the payment happened. No StablePay API needed to verify this — it's on-chain, public, permanent.

### Verifying your wallet balance

You can also check your wallet balance directly on any Solana block explorer or wallet app. The USDC in your wallet is the sum of all incoming SPL transfers minus any outgoing ones. This is independent of what StablePay's API reports.

## Refunds, disputes, and erroneous payments

### Current state

StablePay v1 does **not** support on-chain refunds. Payments are irreversible SPL transfers — once confirmed on Solana, they cannot be reversed by StablePay.

### What to do

| Scenario | Recommended action |
|----------|-------------------|
| Buyer paid by mistake | Manually send USDC back to the buyer's wallet from yours |
| Buyer disputes a charge | Resolve off-chain. If you choose to refund, send USDC to their wallet. Keep records of the refund tx_hash |
| Buyer paid wrong amount | Refund the difference manually |
| Duplicate payment (409) | This is prevented by idempotency. If it occurs, the second payment is rejected with `20002` |

<Info>
  **Always keep your own records.** For any refund you issue, save both the original purchase `tx_hash` and your refund `tx_hash`. This is your paper trail for tax and dispute resolution.
</Info>

### Future

On-chain refund mechanisms and dispute resolution are under consideration for a future release. If you have requirements in this area, share them with the StablePay team.

## Tax and compliance

<Warning>
  **StablePay is a payment protocol, not a tax advisor.** The information below is general guidance. Consult a qualified accountant or tax professional for your specific situation.
</Warning>

### What you're responsible for

StablePay facilitates the transfer of USDC from buyer to seller. As a developer receiving payments:

- **Income reporting**: Payments you receive are likely taxable income in your jurisdiction. Keep records of all sales (use `/api/v1/sales` to export them).
- **Indirect tax (VAT/GST/sales tax)**: Whether you need to charge and remit tax depends on your location, the buyer's location, and the nature of your service. StablePay does not calculate, collect, or remit taxes on your behalf.
- **Transaction records**: The Solana blockchain provides a permanent, public record of every transfer. `tx_hash` values are your proof of payment. StablePay's API provides additional metadata (agent DID, skill DID, timestamps) that link on-chain transfers to specific purchases.

### What StablePay provides

- Immutable on-chain transaction records (via Solana)
- Purchase metadata via the API (`tx_id`, `tx_hash`, timestamps, agent/skill DIDs)
- Sales and revenue summaries via `/api/v1/sales` and `/api/v1/revenue`

### Data export

Use `/api/v1/sales` and `/api/v1/revenue` to export your transaction history for accounting purposes. For a permanent record, save the `tx_hash` of every payment — it's your immutable proof on Solana.
